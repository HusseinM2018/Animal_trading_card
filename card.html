Horses

Horses can rest and sleep while standing upright by locking one of their hind legs at the stifle joint, which is similar to a human knee. They can occasionally switch the locked leg to avoid fatigue.

  • Scientific name: Equus caballus
  • he average length: 8 to 9 feet
  • Average Lifespan: 20 to 30 years
  • Habitat: steppes and open grasslands

The horse is a domesticated, one-toed, hoofed mammal. It belongs to the taxonomic family Equine and is one of two extant subspecies of Equus fetus. The horse has evolved over the past 45 to 55 million years from a small multi-toed creature, close to Eohippus, into the large, single-toed animal of today.
